## Key Components

### 1. Dealer Positioning + Options Flow

This is where flacko differentiates from typical TA services. TSLA is one of the most option-driven stocks in the market. Market makers hedge their options exposure, creating:

- **Gamma walls** â€” Price levels where dealers must buy/sell heavily
- **Magnets** â€” Levels price gravitates toward
- **Squeezes** â€” Forced buying/selling cascades
- **Air pockets** â€” Low-liquidity zones

Flacko integrates SpotGamma dealer flow data to identify these levels.

### 2. The Rulebook

Two non-negotiables enforced by the system:

**Slow Zone:** When signals are mixed, you don't trade. Period. Most drawdowns start as "confusion trades."

**Master Eject:** When structure breaks AND dealer positioning flips, you stop averaging down. You hedge or exit.

## Honest Assessment

### What's Good

**1. The core insight is correct.** Most retail TSLA traders don't have an *edge* problem â€” they have a *discipline* problem. Removing the human from "just one more dip" decisions is the right framework.

**2. Dealer flow is real alpha.** Options positioning genuinely moves TSLA. Most retail traders ignore this entirely. SpotGamma integration adds value.

**3. Mode-based sizing is smart.** The 4-mode system forces position sizing to scale with conditions, which prevents the averaging-into-oblivion death spiral.

**4. It's designed for execution, not analysis paralysis.** Daily outputs are actionable: mode, levels, rules. Not 40-page theses.

### Concerns

**1. Subscription cost vs. alternative approaches.** For traders with significant TSLA positions, a simple collar strategy (buy protective put, sell covered call) provides mechanical drawdown protection at near-zero cost. No daily analysis needed.

**2. Active trading requires active attention.** Intraday alerts mean you need to be available to execute. This works for full-time traders, not someone with a day job who checks occasionally.

**3. Dealer flow data has noise.** SpotGamma and similar services are valuable, but gamma levels aren't precise â€” they're estimates based on OI and assumptions about dealer positioning. False signals happen.

**4. Track record is unclear.** The thread is well-written and the framework is sound, but I didn't find public backtests or verified P&L.

### My Take

If you're a **long-term TSLA holder** who doesn't want to trade actively, a collar strategy is simpler and more reliable. Buy a put 10-15% OTM, sell a call 20-30% OTM, pay near-zero net premium, sleep at night.

If you're an **active swing trader** who wants to capitalize on TSLA volatility (not just survive it), flacko's framework is solid. The 4-mode system + dealer flow integration is more sophisticated than most retail approaches.
