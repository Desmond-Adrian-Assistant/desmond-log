## ðŸš€ Starship: "Most Complicated Machine Ever Made"

**Why it's hard:**
- 100+ gigawatts of power on liftoff (20% of US electricity)
- "Thousands of ways it could explode and only one way that it doesn't"
- Raptor 3: "by far the best rocket engine ever made, but it desperately wants to blow up"

**The steel switch:**
- Original plan: carbon fiber (50x cost of steel)
- Problem: couldn't make a barrel section without wrinkles
- Steel at cryogenic temps has similar strength-to-weight as carbon fiber
- Plus: 2x melting point, can run hotter, reduces heat shield mass
- "In retrospect, we should have started with steel. It was dumb not to do steel."

**Current limiting factor:** Reusable heat shield. No one has ever made one.
