## The Bear Case (Stay Honest)

No thesis is complete without stress-testing it:

1. **AI plateau risk.** Model improvements slow. Agents remain too unreliable for autonomous action. The "last 20%" of reliability proves intractable.
2. **Regulation kills it.** EU AI Act, potential US regulation. If agents can't autonomously transact, the thesis stalls.
3. **Privacy backlash.** One major breach of an AI agent with access to someone's bank accounts and email could set adoption back years.
4. **Compute costs stay high.** If running a personal agent costs $50-100/month, it remains a premium product, not universal.
5. **Incumbents adapt.** Google builds ads into agent responses. Apple makes Siri actually competent. The disruption gets absorbed.
