## What Is the Hindenburg Omen?

The Hindenburg Omen is a technical indicator designed to predict potential stock market crashes. Named after the 1937 Hindenburg airship disaster, it signals market instability when certain breadth conditions are met simultaneously.

### The Criteria (All Must Be True)

1. **New Highs + New Lows > 2.8%** of advancing + declining issues on NYSE
2. **New 52-week highs cannot be more than 2x new 52-week lows**
3. **NYSE Composite is in an uptrend** (10-week moving average rising)
4. **McClellan Oscillator is negative**

When all four trigger together, the market is showing internal divergence â€” stocks are hitting new highs AND new lows simultaneously in unusual numbers. This internal chaos supposedly precedes major corrections.

## Historical Track Record

**The Good:**
- Preceded the 2008 Financial Crisis
- Triggered before the 2015-2016 correction
- Signaled before the 2020 COVID crash

**The Bad:**
- Massive false positive rate (~75-80% of signals don't lead to crashes)
- Many clusters occur during normal bull market volatility
- The 1987 crash had no Hindenburg warning

**The Ugly:**
- Cherry-picking issue: proponents highlight hits, ignore misses
- Definition varies (different analysts use slightly different thresholds)
- Doesn't specify timing (crash in days? weeks? months?)
